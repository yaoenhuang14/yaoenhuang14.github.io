Psychology

My name is Yao En Huang, and I am a first-year psychology major currently attending the University of California, Riverside. I have a strong passion for understanding human behavior, cognition, and workplace dynamics, which has led me to explore the field of Industrial-Organizational (I/O) Psychology as my intended career path. Additionally, I am in the process of adding a marketing minor to complement my studies, as I believe having knowledge in both psychology and marketing will provide me with a well-rounded skill set applicable to various professional settings. Beyond academics, I am actively involved in several student organizations that align with both my academic and personal interests. I am currently a mentee and prospective member of Psi Chi, the Psychology Honor Society, where I have the opportunity to connect with like-minded peers and mentors who share a passion for psychology. This experience has been invaluable in helping me navigate my academic journey while gaining insight into future opportunities within the field.

In addition to Psi Chi, I am also a member of Tartan Seoul, the K-pop dance team on campus. Dancing has always been a significant part of my life, and being able to participate in a team setting allows me to express myself while building meaningful connections with fellow dancers. I am also involved in the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) and the Taiwanese American Student Association (TASA), where I engage with cultural events, social gatherings, and community-building activities. These organizations provide me with a sense of belonging and an opportunity to celebrate my heritage while connecting with students from diverse backgrounds. When I am not studying or participating in extracurricular activities, I enjoy spending my free time dancing, singing, and listening to music. Music has always been a source of inspiration and relaxation for me, and I love exploring different genres and artists. I also value quality time with friends, whether it’s through casual hangouts, movie nights, or going out to try new restaurants and experiences. These moments allow me to maintain a balanced lifestyle and create lasting memories with the people around me.

Looking ahead, I am excited about the next steps in my academic and professional journey. I currently have sophomore standing and will reach junior standing by next quarter. As I continue to progress, I am considering joining a research lab or applying for internships to gain hands-on experience in psychology and organizational behavior. Additionally, I am contemplating studying abroad in Korea during my second-year spring quarter, which would allow me to immerse myself in a new culture while expanding my global perspective. Ultimately, my goal is to apply to graduate school and pursue a career as an Industrial-Organizational Psychologist. I am eager to gain practical experience, develop my skills, and continue growing both academically and personally. The opportunities and experiences I am currently exploring at UCR will undoubtedly shape my future, and I look forward to the journey ahead.
